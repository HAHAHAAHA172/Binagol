Binagol



source:EV Learners


Binagol is a Filipino sweet steamed delicacy of the Waray people made from mashed giant taro corms, condensed milk, sugar, coconut milk, and egg yolks. It is distinctively placed in half of a coconut shell and then wrapped in banana leaves and twine. The name means "to place in a coconut shell", from the Visayan bagol, "coconut shell". Binagol traditionally uses the corms of the giant taro (locally known as talyan or talian); however, the corms of taro (known in Tagalog as gabi and in the Eastern Visayas, where the delicacy originates, as gaway) is also alternatively used. It is a type of nilupak.


Binagol is a sweet and delicious delicacy that originated in Leyte. It offers a sticky and sweet taste to the pallet and it is covered with unique leaf packaging. Binagol originally came from the word “bagol”, a Visayan language that means coconut shell.

INGREDIENTS

    3/4 cups shredded gabi
    1 cup rich coconut milk
    3/4 cup brown sugar
    4 clean medium sized coconut shells
    1/2 can 1 oz full cream condensed milk
    4 egg yolks
    Wilted banana leaves
    String for tying


Procedures

    1. Mix first three ingredients and cook over moderate heat for 6 minutes, constantly stirring.
    2. Lower heat and continue cooking for 10 minutes.
    3. Add condensed milk and cook over low heat 20 minutes longer, stirring constantly.
    4. Fill each coconut shell with mixture. Make a well in center and drop raw egg yolk.
    5. Cover top with tuber mixture and spread until smooth, very close to brim or shell.
    6. Cover whole shell with two layers banana leaves and tie securely with strings.
    7. Steam half an hour 12 servings.
